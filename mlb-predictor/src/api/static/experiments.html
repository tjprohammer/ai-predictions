Model Experiments

How accurate are our predictions compared to what actually happened?

How to read this page: We train a machine learning model on real baseball stats to predict game totals (combined runs scored) and individual pitcher strikeouts. The question is: does blending in the Vegas line make our predictions better, or can the stats alone beat the market? The key number is Avg Miss — how far off each prediction was from the real outcome. Lower is better. An avg miss of 1.50 means our prediction was typically 1.5 runs (or Ks) away from what actually happened.
Stats-Only Our model's raw prediction using only baseball data — no Vegas influence at all. This is what the model thinks will happen based purely on the numbers. Totals: offensive rates, starter matchups (xwOBA, CSW, velo, whiff%), bullpen workload & ERA, lineup quality, park factors, weather. Strikeouts: pitcher K rates, recent form (3/5 game), opponent lineup K%, handedness splits, venue K factor. Blended Takes the Stats-Only prediction and adjusts it toward the Vegas line using a calibration model. The idea is that the market carries information our stats might miss (injuries, weather changes, sharp money). Uses the same stats model as Stats-Only, then feeds both the raw prediction and the Vegas line into a second model that decides how much weight to give each. Vegas The sportsbook's over/under line (totals) or prop line (strikeouts). This is our benchmark — can our model beat the house?
